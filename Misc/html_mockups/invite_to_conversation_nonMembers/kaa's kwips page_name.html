Wow--good stuff! Great discussion, guys! I almost don't want to pick sides, but I'm afraid I already did--I just posted this to Kaa's blog: http://thepete.com/10-points/

That's a post I wrote a while back about how statistically odds are none of us in America will ever be killed by terrorists, yet we act like they are a major threat to our country. Yes, of course, they are a danger and should be stopped, but living in fear does mean the terrorists win.

We Americans are raised (well, we're supposed to be raised) with the idea that freedom is worth dying for. If you live in fear, that's not freedom. We should be willing to take a few hits for our freedoms every once in a while.

It's interesting that Kaa used some of the same examples as I did in my post--car accidents are a great comparison. I also used cancer and AIDS. The point being is that all three things kill astronomically more people in each of our countries than terrorists do worldwide.

Yet, we're only outraged when people are blown up.

Cancer seems to kill someone famous in this country every week. We just lost an amazing actor and humanitarian, Paul Newman, to cancer. My step-dad is a cancer survivor. Ted Kennedy has a brain tumor.

Cancer is everywhere, but we treat it like it's less controllable than a guy who gets pissed off at a government and blows something up.

The fact is that all of these deaths can be predicted, stopped and cured if enough money is spent on each. But shouldn't we have priorities? And shouldn't we have responses that are measured to the reality of the threats?

Check it out--I dropped the odds of me dying in a car accident dramatically--How? By moving to the number 1 terrorist target in the US. I don't own a car anymore but I live in NYC. Which am I more scared of?

I think you're both right to say that you shouldn't be returning to normal after a bombing and you shouldn't be hanging around in case of multiple bombs being placed or, god forbid, if they used a dirty bomb, but like Kaa says at the end of his post: "...if you feel terrorized the terrorists succeed, remember that."
